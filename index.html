About

I am a researcher at the Computational Biomedicine Lab, University of Houston
working with Prof. Ioannis Kakadiaris on data analysis, computer-human interaction,
text/image analysis, cybersecurity, and social media analysis.
I have a PhD in Computer Science (University of Athens), an MPhil in Computation (UMIST),
an MA in Linguistics (University of Manchester) and a BSc in Computation (UMIST).

Social Media Analysis during Political Turbulence, PLoS ONE, 2017

An in-depth exploratory analysis of Twitter posts for two majot political events, a referendum and the follow-up elections revealed hidden aspects of public sentiment towards political actors. This work combined five types of analysis: volume analysis, entity identification, sentiment detection, sarcasm correction and topic analysis. Major findings include the co-enhancement of results between the types of analysis and the quantitative aspects of data analytics visualized for social and political interpretations. Worries, fears and other sentiments provide insight to the mindset of the citizens as they were called upon to decide on their future. read more
